Solutions to PS2, EC569 Economic Growth (University of Kent)¶

1 Wakanda and Zakanda are two neighboring countries. Wakanda has a highly advanced technology, whereas, Zakanda has a low level of technology and low income per capita. In the usual setting, we would expect Zakanda to learn about Wakanda's technology but Wakanda has been successfully hiding its technology from the rest of the world for centuries. At time $t=0$, Wakanda has a productivity of 10, $A_w = 10$, and 20% of its labor force engage in R\&D, $\gamma_{A,W} = .2$. Zakanda, on the other hand, has a productivity level of 1, $A_Z=1$, and 5% of its population engages in R&D, $\gamma_{A,Z}=.05$. Each country has a labor force equal to 1, $L=1$. Price of invention in each country is equal to 10, $\mu = 10$. Along the line, the following events take place:

  • After long soul searching, at time $t=10$, King T'Challa of Wakanda decides to open up Wakanda's technology to Zakanda.
  • King T'Chala observes Zakanda's development for 10 periods. But, he is surprised that Zakanda did not imitate Wakanda's technology and it grows at its former pace, as before time $t=10$. He appoints his sister and Chief Scientist of Wakanda, Shuri, as his technology ambassador to Zakanda at time $t=20$.
  • With the help of Shuri, Zakanda is able to imitate Wakanda's technology with a cost of copying equal to $\mu_{c} = \mu_i \left(\frac{A_W}{A_Z} \right)^{-1/2}.$ Shuri, who is highly intelligent and witty, convinces Zakanda to increase the fraction of workers engaged in R&D to 10%, $\gamma_{A,Z}=.10$

Solution¶

Notice that until $t=20$, Wakanda's technology is hidden from Zakanda. Hence, we need to consider technological progress in each of the countries separately as our one country model. Therefore, to calculate Wakanda's productivity and income over time, first we calculate productivity growth rate:

$$ \hat{A_w}(t) = \frac{\gamma_{A,w} L }{\mu}$$

Then, we calculate output:

$$ y_{w}(t) = A_w(t)(1-\gamma_{A,w}) $$

Then, using the growth rate we calculated and current productivity level, we can calculate next period's productivity:

$$ A_w(t+1) = A_w(t)(1+\hat{A_w}(t))$$

Starting from $t=0$, we repeat these calculations 20 times to get productivity and income of Wakanda for $t=0,20$

We calculate Zakanda's productivity and income until t=19 as we did for Wakanda:

$$ \hat{A_z}(t) = \frac{\gamma_{A,z} L }{\mu}$$$$ y_{z}(t) = A_z(t)(1-\gamma_{A,z}) $$$$ A_z(t+1) = A_z(t)(1+\hat{A_z}(t))$$

Repeate the above calculations 19 times.

Now, at time $t=20$, we observe 2 important developments. First, Zakanda begins imitating Wakanda's technology, and second, Zakanda increases share of labor force engaged in R&D to .1, notated as $\gamma_{A,z}^n$, where $n$ represents 'new'. However, as Wakanda is the technology leader, nothing changes for Wakanda, and we can keep calculating Wakanda's income and output as we did before until $t=40$. Now, Zakanda's productivity growth can be calculated as follows:

First, calculate the cost of imitation at time $t$, $$\mu_{c}(t) = \mu \left(\frac{A_w(t)}{A_z(t)} \right)^{-1/2}$$

Notice that cost of imitation depends on the ratio of productivities in these countries. Hence, we need to calcualte a new cost of imitation at each time $t$. Then we can calculate productivity growth rate in Zakanda,

$$ \hat{A_z}(t) = \frac{\gamma_{A,z}^n L }{\mu_c(t)}$$

Lastly, we can can calculate output in Zakanda,

$$ y_{z}(t) = A_z(t)(1-\gamma_{A,z}^n) $$

Lastly we need to repeat this from $t=20$ to $t=40$.

# this is the algorithm I described above
for t in range(T-1):
    A_w_hat[t] = gamma_w[t]*L/mu
    A_w[t+1] = A_w[t]*(1+A_w_hat[t])
    if t<20:
        A_z_hat[t] = gamma_z[t]*L/mu
    else:
        mu_c = mu*(A_w[t]/A_z[t])**(-1/2)
        A_z_hat[t] = gamma_z[t]*L/mu_c
    A_z[t+1] = A_z[t]*(1+A_z_hat[t])   

y_w = (1-gamma_w)*A_w # output in Wakanda
y_z = (1-gamma_z)*A_z # output in Zakanda

Solution: First, for each country, calculate income per worker and capital stock per worker by dividing 'cgdpe' and 'ck' by 'emp' (employment). Second, divide income per worker, capital per worker, human capital per worker by the Great Britain values.

Lastly, calculate productivity differences as follows: $$\frac{A_i}{A_{GBR}}=\frac{\frac{y_i}{y_{GBR}}}{\frac{k_i^\alpha h_i^{1-\alpha}}{k_{GBR}^\alpha h_{GBR}^{1-\alpha}}}$$

The variables we use are

cgdpe: Expenditure-side real GDP at current PPPs (in mil. 2011US\$)

ck: Capital stock at current PPPs (in mil. 2011US\$)

4. Using the Penn World Table, conduct growth accounting of Italy, Greece, Spain, South Korea and the United Kingdom. Examine data from 1975 to 2014. Use rgdpna and rkna as output and capital stock measures. Create a table (similar to Table 7.3 of the textbook). Briefly comment on the table.

Solution: First, calculate growth rates per year from 1975 to 2014, $$ g_y = \left(\frac{y_{2014}}{y_{1975}} \right)^{1/39}-1 $$ $$ g_k = \left(\frac{k_{2014}}{k_{1975}} \right)^{1/39}-1 $$ $$ g_h = \left(\frac{h_{2014}}{h_{1975}} \right)^{1/39}-1 $$

Assuming $y=A k^\alpha h^{1-\alpha}$, then $g_y = g_A + \alpha g_k + (1-\alpha)g_h$. Therefore, growth rate of productivity is $$g_A = g_y - \alpha g_k - (1-\alpha)g_h$$.

Lastly, contribution of productivity growth to income growth is equal to $g_A/g_y$, contribution of physical capital growth to income growth is $\alpha g_k/g_y$, and contribution of human capital growth to income growth is $(1-\alpha)g_h/g_y$

The variables we use are

rgdpna: Real GDP at constant 2011 national prices (in mil. 2011US\$)

rkna: Capital stock at constant 2011 national prices (in mil. 2011US\$)
